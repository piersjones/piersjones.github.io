Jaguar

Brand News

Recently, Jaguar have established quite an unexpected partnership with a 22-year-old pop icon in order to push their latest SUV model…

Confused? Don’t worry, so was I.

In an attempt to target a younger and more progressive audience, this luxury car brand have partnered with the British-Kosovan artist, Dua Lipa.

Dua will not only perform a duet with the new Jaguar I-PACE (yes, a duet with a car) but an app will also be available for her fans to remix her latest track ‘Want To’ according to their Jaguar I-PACE driving data.

Apparently over a million (very expensive) remixes are available.

Isn’t that wheely cool?!

Channel 4

Brand News

In this day and age, hate speech is an everyday occurrence that Channel 4 have decided to face head on, stating that remaining silent ultimately lets the internet trolls win.

Through overlaying the latest ad campaigns from Nationwide, Mars and McCain with the hateful comments made about them online, Channel 4 are attempting to show the real-life impacts of hate speech.

It’s pretty horrific seeing what keyboard warriors say online and the ‘edited’ ads are definitely worth checking out.

Facebook

Tech News

Despite Facebook’s reputation decline this year, the creation of an AI that helps prevent suicide appears to be a step in the right direction

As social platforms have become such a personal space for many to discuss their emotions and lives, Facebook have recently begun to use machine learning to identify concerning posts and concerned comments from users.

Regardless of the multiple language nuances to consider, Facebook believes that the alert of phrases like ‘Tell me where you are’ and ‘Has anyone heard from him/her’ is one step closer to offering individuals the support they need.

Good job Mark.

Snapchat

Tech News

In classic Snapchat fashion, the social app is offering their publishers new ways to not only make stories, but also money.

Snapchat publishers such as Refinery29 and Daily Mail will now be allowed to build stories from the videos created by Snap’s 188 million daily users.

But how does it work? Well, Snap publishers will have access to the app’s content management system which will basically allow them to search through the public videos uploaded.

Publishers will even be able to search through themes and locations to potentially build stories around anything and everything!

YouTube

Digital Platform News

With 70% of YouTube watching time happening on mobile, this video-sharing platform is finally incorporating vertical video ads on their channel in order to let mobile users have a more seamless experience!

Ads will now fill up a user’s screen similar to the likes of Snapchat and Instagram.

The ads you’re presented with will also be personalised and based around content on your home feed.
